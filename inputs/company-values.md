# Reap Values — "The Reap 5"

## What are values?

Values define the soul of the company. They define how we want each one of our team members at Reap to work and behave. These values are linked to performance and reward and recognition decisions.

- **Core values**: rules/guiding principles that define Reap's culture and personality. These values aren't created, they're here already.
- **Aspirational values**: values we want to have, but still need to develop as a company.

---

## 1. Own the Outcome [aspirational]

At Reap, the scoreboard tracks impact, not chair-time. We presume trust from day one, so when you take on a problem you also own its finish line — whether that's a delighted customer, a revenue spike, or a smoother internal process. **We have a bias to action**: you get what you need to pull forward, pull in teammates when needed, and close the loop; excuses and "almosts" don't ship. **Quality ships** in every line of code, support email, and pitch deck that carries the Reap badge, because your work is your signature.

**Ownership has no fence lines here.** If you spot a blocker outside your job description, you clear it. If a chance to raise the bar appears, you seize it rather than waiting for permission, but you do it with the whole company in mind. Acting in silos, guarding turf, or building personal kingdoms runs counter to true ownership. **Real leaders seek the best outcome for Reap**, not just for their team or project, and that mindset goes hand in hand with humility: **nothing is beneath you** when the mission matters.

Sweeping the floors, rewriting a query, or jumping on a late-night call with a client are all the same act: making Reap better today than it was yesterday. Own the outcome. Finish strong. Then look up and ask, "What else can we make possible?"

---

## 2. Start with the Customer [aspirational]

**Reap exists only if we solve real problems for real people**, so our first move always points outward. We stay relentlessly curious — sitting beside a client while they issue a new card, poring over support tickets, or texting a CFO to learn what "good" looks like in their world. Listening is just the beginning. We keep digging until we understand the why beneath each request, because **our job is to deliver what unlocks the most value for the customers** we're built to serve — not to rubber-stamp every feature wish-list.

**Curiosity meets discipline in validation.** Before code is merged or policy written, we run experiments with actual users, scrutinise the data, and ask: "Does this move the metric that matters to them *and* to the business?" If the answer is no, we cut and move on; if it's yes, we double down and polish until the experience feels effortless.

Great products earn affection. When a customer says, "Reap just gets it," that's our true north and the surest sign we started in the right place.

---

## 3. Speak Up, Team Up [aspirational]

We build better, faster, and smarter when everyone at Reap is both a committed teammate and a thoughtful challenger. Collaboration is our default setting: Slack pings get answered, documents are open for comments, information is shared liberally, and no one hides behind titles — senior or new joiner, we show up for one another when help is needed.

Yet partnership without pushback breeds average. So we pair humility with backbone. We assume we might not be the most knowledgeable person in the room, which keeps us eager for feedback and fresh perspectives. At the same time, we owe the team our own thinking — silence helps no customer. If you spot a blind spot, you surface it; if you have an idea, you put it on the table. Debate the idea, respect the person.

When collaboration meets candid challenge, the best solution wins — and our customers and business reap the rewards. We didn't hire robots to nod along; we hired people to think, share, and raise the bar together.

---

## 4. Think Deep, Move Fast [aspirational]

**Speed is our competitive oxygen**: the market will not wait while we perfect every plan. When an opportunity opens, we act — shipping, iterating, unblocking teammates, and answering customers with startup-level urgency. Sometimes that means accepting more risk than a mature giant would tolerate; it is the price of seizing a once-in-a-lifetime window.

Yet velocity without depth is reckless. **Before we sprint, we pause long enough to understand the real problem, crunch the data, and listen across teams.** We document the decision path so future teammates can build on it, not rediscover it. When we do ship a quick patch, we log the debt and schedule the fix; root causes get solved, corners don't stay cut. If a task can be automated, we automate it — freeing people to think, not click.

**We treat every misstep as fuel for improvement**, extracting lessons fast enough to make the next release better. Moving fast wins the moment; thinking deep wins the decade. We do both, every day.

---

## 5. Anything is Possible [core]

**Reap was born by walking into markets that incumbents labeled "too hard."** We still run toward that complexity. When others see a wall, we look for the door — or cut one. The reflex is optimism first: *what would it take?* not *why won't it work?* That mindset turns impossible card flows, cross-border constraints, or licensing mazes into shipped products and grateful customers.

Anything is possible is not wishful thinking; it is disciplined invention. **We pair the freedom to launch new ideas with the responsibility to keep them aligned to Reap's strategy.** If a proposal moves our north-star metrics and fits the customer we serve, we green-light and go. If it doesn't, we iterate or park it — and redirect our energy. Autonomy thrives inside a clear lane.

Frontier first-movers stay first by delivering, not just dreaming. So **we set bold goals, surface blockers early, and push** until the new capability is live, measured, and scaling. Complexity is our playground; possibility is the output.
