# Reap Job Architecture Framework

> Source: [Shared] Reap_Job Architecture_Framework_V1_Team Heads_Leads Reference

## Purpose

Job levels describe the **scope, accountability, and impact** of roles — not individual performance, tenure, or personal attributes. Levels describe impact and accountability — not effort, tenure, or title.

## How to Read Levels (in this order)

1. **Level (L1–L12)** — overall role weight
2. **Job Band (P / M / E)** — how impact is created
3. **Description** — why the role exists
4. **Scope** — how broad the responsibility is
5. **Core Competencies** — how success is achieved
6. **Impact Descriptor** — type of value created
7. **Impact Test** — validation question

## Three Job Bands

| Band | Code | How Impact is Created |
|------|------|----------------------|
| **Professional (P)** | P1–P7 | Through individual expertise and execution. Progression = more complex problems, broader influence. Senior ICs may influence decisions but do not own people outcomes. |
| **Managerial (M)** | M1–M5 | Through others. Accountable for delivery, capability, and results. If success depends on personal output, the role is not managerial. |
| **Executive (E)** | E1–E6 | Through direction and enterprise decisions. Decisions shape functions, businesses, or the company. Accountability is long-term and often irreversible. |

## Full Level Definitions

### L1 — P1: Entry Professional

**Description:** Early career professional learning processes, systems, and industry fundamentals. Works under close supervision.

**Scope:** Entry-level role. Performs routine tasks and assists in projects, building foundational knowledge. May rotate across different tasks to build exposure.

**Core Competencies:** Willingness to learn and adapt. Basic functional skills. Team collaboration and reliability.

**Impact:** Learning & Task Impact — delivers assigned tasks accurately while learning processes, tools, and standards.

**Impact Test:** Can this person complete tasks correctly with guidance?

**Sample Roles:** Associate, Assistant

---

### L2 — P2: Skilled Professional

**Description:** Mid-level professional responsible for executing operational, compliance, or customer-facing tasks with precision and timeliness. Follows established processes, ensures standards are met, resolves issues within their scope.

**Scope:** Executes operational or administrative tasks within a specific function. Works under supervision of a Senior Officer, Lead, or Manager. Independent within set processes.

**Core Competencies:** Process adherence and operational efficiency. Stakeholder communication. Problem escalation and resolution. Functional knowledge and skill application. Quality control and attention to detail.

**Impact:** Reliable Execution Impact — executes operational or technical work independently within established processes.

**Impact Test:** Can we rely on this person to deliver without supervision?

**Sample Roles:** Officer, Specialist, Analyst

---

### L3 — P3: Advanced Professional

**Description:** Experienced professional handling complex tasks with minimal supervision. Often acts as a mentor to junior team members and may lead projects without formal people management responsibilities. Process-driven, ensures compliance and operational delivery.

**Scope:** Performs specialized work within a defined process or sub-function. Works with moderate independence, escalating complex issues. Provides guidance to junior officers or associates.

**Core Competencies:** Advanced technical or business expertise. Project coordination and mentoring. Process improvement and best practice application. Independent problem-solving. Specialized knowledge application.

**Impact:** Problem-Solving Impact — solves non-routine problems and improves execution within a defined scope.

**Impact Test:** Can they handle complexity and ambiguity independently?

**Sample Roles:** Sr. Officer, Sr. Specialist, Sr. Analyst

---

### L4 — M1: First-Line Manager / P4: Senior Professional (Lead IC)

**M1 — First-Line Manager:**
- Oversees a small to medium-sized team or sub-function (3–7 direct reports)
- Balances hands-on work with people leadership
- Handles performance evaluations, coaching, daily workflows
- Building leadership capability for future Manager roles

**Impact Test:** If the manager stops working hands-on, does the team still deliver?

**P4 — Senior Professional / Lead IC:**
- Acts as a senior point of expertise within a domain
- Guides project delivery, mentors team members, ensures technical/procedural quality
- No formal direct reports, but provides technical direction
- Deep subject-matter expertise, technical decision-making, peer mentoring

**Impact Test:** Do others rely on this person's judgment to get it right?

**Sample Roles:** Lead, IC Lead, Team Lead

---

### L5 — M2: Manager / P5: Expert IC

**M2 — Manager:**
- Leads a single team or unit within a function (5–10 direct reports)
- Accountable for operational KPIs, project delivery, and employee development
- Primary coach and evaluator for team members
- Bridge between senior management and frontline employees
- Drives talent development and succession planning

**Impact Test:** Do outcomes depend on how this person structures teams and work?

**P5 — Expert / Principal IC:**
- Recognized subject-matter expert or thought leader
- Provides strategic guidance, acts as technical authority
- Influences decision-making without holding direct people management
- Impact is enterprise-wide or market-level, even without formal direct reports
- Leads complex, cross-functional, or enterprise-wide initiatives

**Impact Test:** Do leaders change decisions because of this person's input?

**Sample Roles:** Manager, Principal IC

---

### L6 — M3: Senior Manager / P6: Senior Expert

**M3 — Senior Manager:**
- Mid-level leadership within a function, bridge between Director/Head (L7) and Managers (L5)
- Manages multiple managers or senior individual contributors
- Accountable for functional execution, project delivery, operational performance
- Acts as delegate for Director/Head when needed
- Scope: typically 10–20 team members across several workstreams

**Impact Test:** Does this role determine how the function runs?

**P6 — Senior Expert:**
- Senior expert and recognized authority in a specialized domain
- Shapes standards, guides strategic decisions
- Develops and governs best practices, frameworks, methodologies
- Mentors senior professionals and experts (P4–P5)
- Internal advisor and external representative for specialized knowledge

**Impact Test:** Does this role set direction others must follow?

**Sample Roles:** Director, Head of Function

---

### L7 — E1 / M4 / P7

**E1 — Enterprise Leader:**
- Accountable for shaping direction, priorities, and outcomes beyond a single team or function
- Sets strategy on behalf of the enterprise
- Decisions have enterprise-wide and often irreversible impact

**M4 — Country Lead / Head of Function:**
- Leads operations and growth within a specific country during early/scaling phase OR provides strategic and operational leadership for a single function
- Manages multiple teams through Senior Managers or Managers
- Owns functional KPIs, operating plans, and budgets

**P7 — Distinguished Expert:**
- Highest tier of expertise within the organization
- Defines strategic direction and company-wide standards
- Collaborates with C-Suite, influences corporate strategy
- Represents organization externally at highest levels

**Sample Roles:** Head of LATAM, Head of Africa, Head of Department, Distinguished/Fellow

---

### L8 — E2 / M5

**E2 — Senior Executive:**
- Owns performance and evolution of multiple businesses, markets, or major business platform
- Shapes growth strategy, operating models, and capital allocation

**M5 — Business Leader (Country Manager / General Manager):**
- Leads a single mature country or business unit with full P&L accountability
- All local functions report to them (Sales, Ops, Finance, Compliance, People)
- Primary external representative for customers, regulators, and partners

**Impact Test:** Would the company grow differently without them? / Is this person accountable for business success or failure?

**Sample Roles:** Regional Operations Head, Country Manager, General Manager

---

### L9–L12: Executive Leadership

| Level | Band | Role | Description |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| L9 | E3 | VP | Senior executive providing strategic leadership for major business units, functions, or regions. Bridge between C-Suite and operational leaders. |
| L10 | E4 | C-Suite | Enterprise-wide accountability for strategy execution. Oversees entire corporate functions. Direct authority over VPs. |
| L11 | E5 | President/Deputy CEO | Second-in-command. Enterprise-wide oversight. Ensures strategies are executed across all divisions. |
| L12 | E6 | Co-Founders/CEO | Visionary and ultimate decision-maker. Holds ultimate accountability for the entire enterprise. |

## Growth Stages Within Levels

Within each level, a person progresses through three stages:

### Proficient (Junior within level)
- **Scope:** Handles medium-complexity tasks independently. Requires some guidance for ambiguous projects.
- **Skills:** Solid core craft. Inconsistent advanced skills. Still learning cross-functional influence.
- **Impact:** Good execution. Impact felt mostly at team or squad level. Not yet driving strategy or frameworks.

### Established (Mid within level)
- **Scope:** Owns end-to-end features or research programs. Works independently even in semi-ambiguous situations. Trusted contributor.
- **Skills:** Strong craft in their discipline. Consistent delivery without handholding. Early mentoring of junior roles.
- **Impact:** Drives product-level improvements. Strong stakeholder alignment. Occasionally elevates team standards.

### Mastery (Senior within level)
- **Scope:** Handles ambiguous, complex, cross-functional challenges. Defines frameworks or reusable patterns. Operates at the next level 70% of the time.
- **Skills:** Coaches multiple peers. Leads without authority. Strong strategic thinking. Demonstrates systems-level problem solving.
- **Impact:** Raises standards beyond their team. Influences roadmap decisions. Their work has org-wide ripple effects.

## Role Type Comparison

| Dimension | Executive | Managerial | Professional |
|-----------|-----------|------------|--------------|
| Primary Purpose | Sets direction, ensures long-term success | Translates strategy into action, manages people | Delivers expertise and execution |
| Scope | Enterprise, region, or major BU | Department, function, or cross-functional team | Projects, workstreams, or subject matter areas |
| Decisions | Strategic and directional ("what") | Tactical and operational ("how") | Operational and technical ("doing") |
| Accountability | Organizational results (P&L, growth, compliance) | Team/functional outcomes (delivery, quality) | Individual outputs and contribution |
| Leadership | Leads other leaders | Leads managers or teams | Leads self or projects |
| Financial | Full or partial P&L ownership | Manages functional budgets | Contributes to cost efficiency |
| Time Horizon | Long-term (3–5 years) | Mid-term (1–3 years) | Short-term (project-based) |

## Non-Negotiable Leveling Rules

1. **Level the role, not the individual**
2. **Titles do not determine level**
3. **Same grade can have different scopes**
4. **Promotions occur only when role impact changes, not performance alone**
